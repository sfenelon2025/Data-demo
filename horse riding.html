Horse Riding — Beginner Basics

Learn the essentials: safety gear, mounting, simple cues, and the three basic gaits. This is a lightweight demo page written in plain HTML, CSS, and a pinch of JavaScript.

Safety First

  • Wear a certified riding helmet and proper boots with a heel.
  • Check saddle and girth/cinch for a snug, even fit.
  • Keep hands calm and cues light; never wrap reins around your hand.
  • Stay aware of horse ears, eyes, and tail — they signal mood.

Basic Gaits

  • Walk — slow, four‑beat rhythm.
  • Trot — bouncy two‑beat; post to stay balanced.
  • Canter — smooth three‑beat; sit deep and relaxed.

Simple Riding Cues

  • Go forward: squeeze calves gently, release when the horse moves.
  • Steer: open the rein slightly in the direction you want to go.
  • Stop: sit tall, exhale, close fingers on reins — then soften.

Mounting Steps

  1. Check girth/cinch and stirrup length.
  2. Stand at the horse’s left shoulder, reins in left hand, right hand on the back of the saddle.
  3. Left foot in stirrup, spring up softly, swing right leg over, sit gently.
  4. Put right foot in stirrup, adjust posture: ears–shoulders–hips–heels in line.

Do & Don’t

✅ Do

  • Keep heels down and eyes up.
  • Reward often with a soft rein and voice.
  • Practice patience; horses learn from consistency.

❌ Don’t

  • Yank the reins or kick hard.
  • Stand directly behind a horse.
  • Rush mounting or ignore tack checks.
